Naya Savera Project

The Naya Savera Project Livelihood Project builds on the current Mahila Gram Uddyog Avam Rural Development Foundation initiatives by expanding project outreach to Fateh pur Sikri Block, District Agra, Uttar Pradsesh. The project works on ensuring a better quality of life for poor rural families in Baruali Ahir and shamshabad , specifically women, through various development activities related to livelihoods and social development. As women are the nucleus of rural family units, and addressing their socio-economic issues is imperative to the development of the family. Thus, the over reaching goal of the Project is to shift current formal and informal trends between women and various community and enabling environment actors, which will ultimately be reflected in increase of the household incomes by women managed enterprise and livelihood activities. The Primary goal of Mahila Gram Uddyog in entering into this arrangement is to increase the incomes of significant numbers of Women in Naya Savera Project areas through the provision of support for different type of small enterprises and facilitate in getting a market through MAdhuram Industries . In Naya Savera Project role of Mahila Gram Uddyog is handholding, Training & Market. This will be achieved by establishing CFCs (Common Facilitation Centre) and providing technical and handholding support to run their business smoothly and profitably. The CFCs having 20 – 30 Member each CFC depending upon the number of SHGs would be run by village level under the technical guidance of Mahila Gram Uddyog, Agra and handholding support. Naya Savera Project Will provides an integrated business model of proceesing different Income Generate Activity.

Main Focus Community Development

1. Increase in average real net income of women in UP from project activities

2. Increase in access to credit for the women in the project area

3. Increase in awareness of beneficiary groups on Social, Political and Financial matters

4. Farmer Outreach will improve farmer production practices and ability to respond to specific product and market demands

5. Business Linkages will develop more efficient and profitable relationships with potential buyers (with focus on a variety of distribution channels) and other market actors

6. Women of Substance will attempt to address gender inequality in income distribution by facilitating better access to information, finance and technology to create alternative sources of income for women

Project Purpose

The overreaching purpose of the Mahila Gramuddyog collaboration is to increase income for 2000 SHGs women in the given project area. To achieve this purpose, the programme will address gender inequality, issues of women by providing information, access to finance and technology to create sources of female-managed on-farm income through the process of Raw incense sticks, Ready Made Garments, sanitary Napkin, Dry Spices, Milk Production, Dari Enterprises. Building of Implementation module in the women Stance component Mahila Gramuddyog of the programme will:

  • Empower women to have a greater influence in Agarbatti business module, e.g., finance, training, marketing, etc.
  • Provide training through a network of both local and outside partners.
  • Offer training on topics such as accessing finance, social & political issues, sustainable given module (Agarbatti).
  • Support diversification into more activities like this.
  • Facilitate access to credit and savings through self-help groups.

Outcome Indicator

The major outcome of the programme includes:

  • Average increase in income of women beneficiaries attributed to programme activities measured against their current income from enterprises and other activities in the future for them.

Organizational Capacity

Mahila Gramuddyog is working in Agra district of U.P. for the empowerment of women through the Agarbatti Module for SHG. It is a nonprofit organization providing technical support to rural poor, educational institutions in the slums of Agra. The organization designs interventions that empower value chain actors to strengthen long-term linkages among themselves efficiently and profitably, responding to market opportunities with its well-performing staff.

Main Objective

The society undertakes the promotion of rural producers' organizations and assists them with technical and management assistance by imparting specialized skills, information, and training related to administration, organization, manufacture, production, storage, process, finance systems and accounts, personnel, marketing, distribution, promotion, research and development, diversification, and expansion. This service is rendered to any persons, firms, companies, trusts, cooperatives, associations, institutions, societies, bodies corporate, government departments, public or local authorities. Other objectives include:

  • Promote universalization of primary education, especially for girls.
  • Empower women by promoting literacy and employment generation programs.
  • Support farmers in agricultural activities leading to economic growth and modernization.
  • Generate awareness about health, sanitation, family welfare, and scientific temper among rural folk.
  • Promote awareness of environmental issues such as forest conservation, rainwater harvesting, alternative energy resources, and pollution control.
  • Promote social justice by extending help to the physically handicapped, child laborers, and orphans.
  • Promote information and communication technologies in the rural sector.
  • Conduct research and create a reliable database to support development strategies and activities.

Expected Output

  • Introduction of new trade for livelihood and imparting new skills among the poor women folk.
  • Steps towards self-reliance with increased confidence, knowledge, and group cohesiveness.
  • Assured income from an assured market.
  • Each beneficiary earning an approximate sum of Rs 125 – Rs. 150 per day.
  • Beneficiaries graduating in terms of knowledge and skills for better and higher production.
  • Regular income source for supplementing family income.
  • Better economic conditions for beneficiaries.
  • Maximizing yield with low-cost inputs.
  • Establishing a systematic cluster-based approach for further replication and expansion.
